Sarpashirsha

A dedicated page for the Sarpashirsha mudra, showing its meaning, traditional uses, and an image placeholder for the hand gesture.

Meaning & Symbolism

Sarpashirsha is often described as "Snake hood." This gesture can represent:

  • Snake
  • sprinkling water
  • offering

In Bharatanatyam, the Sarpashirsha mudra helps the dancer convey simple objects and emotional states with clarity. The shape of the hand becomes a precise symbol that supports narrative expression.

Usage in Performance

This mudra is commonly used to depict Snake, sprinkling water, offering. It may appear in abhinaya passages where the dancer describes nature, character gestures, or devotional action.

While the hand shape is fixed, the final meaning depends on the accompanying eyes, body posture, and stage context.
