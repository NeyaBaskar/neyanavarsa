Ardhapataka

A dedicated page for the Ardhapataka mudra, showing its meaning, traditional uses, and an image placeholder for the hand gesture.

Meaning & Symbolism

Ardhapataka is often described as "Half flag." This gesture can represent:

  • Leaves
  • knife
  • river bank
  • flag

In Bharatanatyam, the Ardhapataka mudra helps the dancer convey simple objects and emotional states with clarity. The shape of the hand becomes a precise symbol that supports narrative expression.

Usage in Performance

This mudra is commonly used to depict Leaves, knife, river bank, flag. It may appear in abhinaya passages where the dancer describes nature, character gestures, or devotional action.

While the hand shape is fixed, the final meaning depends on the accompanying eyes, body posture, and stage context.
